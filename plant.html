Earth
Earth is our home planet, the only known planet with life. üåç Earth is the third planet from the Sun and our home, unique in the universe for supporting life due to its liquid water, atmosphere, and stable climate. Atmosphere: A dense atmosphere containing nitrogen and oxygen, essential for life and protective from solar radiation and meteoroids. Mars
Mars, the "Red Planet," is the fourth planet from the Sun, known for its iron-rich dust that gives it a rusty appearance. Rocky and Dusty: The surface is a desert-like environment. Atmosphere: It has a thin, tenuous atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide. Mars has two small, irregularly shaped moons: Phobos and Deimos, which may be captured asteroids.
Jupiter
Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System and the fifth planet from the Sun. Gas Giant: Unlike rocky planets like Earth, Jupiter has no solid surface. It is made mostly of hydrogen and helium. Largest Planet: Jupiter is so massive that it has more than twice the mass of all the other planets in the Solar System combined. Saturn
Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun, the second-largest in our solar system, and a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Size and Density: Saturn is roughly 10 times wider than Earth and is the least dense planet in the solar system, so light that it would float in water. Rings: Saturn's rings are the most spectacular in the solar system and are composed of particles ranging from dust-sized grains to mountain-sized chunks of ice and rock. Moons: Saturn has many moons, with Titan being the largest, possessing a thick atmosphere. Enceladus is known for its geysers and potential for life.
Uranus
"Ura planet" likely refers to the planet Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun and the third largest in the solar system. Type: An "ice giant" planet. Size: The third largest planet in our solar system. Rotation: Rotates at a nearly 90-degree angle to its orbit, appearing to spin on its side. Atmosphere: Composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane, with water, ammonia, and methane ices making up most of the planet. Temperature: It is the coldest planet in the solar system.
Neptune
Neptune is the eighth and furthest planet from the Sun in our solar system, known as a dark, cold, and windy ice giant with a vivid blue color due to atmospheric methane. Moons: Has numerous moons, including Triton, its largest moon, which orbits backward.
